Korean Fried Chicken

Description

The best Korean fried chicken! Crispy coated buttermilk fried chicken smothered in a spicy Korean inspired gochujang sauce.

The sauce is so rich, flavourful and complex but it comes together very easily. Just throw all the ingredients in a pan and bubble it up. We love to eat this with rice or noodles.

Ingredients

    Chicken and marinade

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 1/4 tsp garlic salt

    • Crispy coating

  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic salt
  • 1/2 tsp celery salt
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • Vegetable oil for frying

    • Sauce

  • 2 tbsp gochujang paste
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 spring onions
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes

Steps

  1. Place the chicken in a bowl. Add the buttermilk, salt, pepper and garlic salt. Mix together, cover and place in the fridge to marinade for at least one hour but up to overnight.
  2. Heat at least 4 cups of oil to a temperature suitable for frying.
  3. Mix the crispy coating ingredients together in a small bowl.
  4. Take the chicken out of the fridge and out of the buttermilk and allow some of the buttermilk to drip off. Dredge the chicken in the crispy coating mixture.
  5. Add the chicken to the hot oil. do not overcrowd the pan.
  6. Cook the chicken for 3-5 minutes until the middle is no longer pink
  7. Make the sauce by mixing all the sauce ingredients in a saucepan. Bring the sauce to a boil then simmer for 5 minutes until thick.
  8. Pour the sauce over the chicken and top with sesame seeds, chili flakes and spring onions.
